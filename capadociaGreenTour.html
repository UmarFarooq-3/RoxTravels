Capadocia Green Tour

The Green Tour in Cappadocia offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, historical exploration, and cultural immersion. Travelers are treated to a diverse array of experiences, from the awe-inspiring landscapes to the ancient underground cities, creating lasting memories of this extraordinary region.

Places to visit in Green Tour
Göreme Panorama:

The Green Tour often begins with a visit to the Göreme Panorama, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the fairy chimneys, unique rock formations, and the enchanting landscape of Cappadocia. This is a perfect introduction to the region's distinctive geological features.

Kaymakli Underground City:

The tour then descends into the fascinating Kaymakli Underground City, one of the many subterranean cities carved into the soft volcanic rock. Kaymakli served as a refuge for early Christians escaping persecution, showcasing an intricate network of tunnels, rooms, and chambers.

Ihlara Valley:

Next on the itinerary is the Ihlara Valley, a lush canyon adorned with towering cliffs and a winding river. Visitors can explore the valley on a leisurely hike along the riverbank, discovering ancient cave churches, rock-cut dwellings, and the serene beauty of nature.

Belisirma Village:

The Green Tour continues to the charming Belisirma Village, where travelers can enjoy a delightful lunch along the riverbanks. This traditional village provides a glimpse into rural Cappadocian life, with its stone houses, vineyards, and friendly locals.

Selime Monastery:

One of the highlights of the tour is the visit to Selime Monastery, an impressive rock-cut complex that served as both a monastery and a cathedral. The monastery offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and showcases remarkable examples of Byzantine rock-cut architecture.

Pigeon Valley:

Pigeon Valley is another natural wonder on the Green Tour, known for its unique rock formations and the thousands of pigeon houses carved into the cliffs. Pigeons played a crucial role in the region's agriculture, and their droppings were used as fertilizer.

Onyx Workshop:

The tour concludes with a visit to an Onyx Workshop, where visitors can witness the intricate craftsmanship of local artisans working with onyx, a type of marble with distinctive layers and colors. This provides an opportunity to purchase unique souvenirs crafted by skilled Cappadocian artists.
